How to help

Whether you’re a homeowner with a yard or just someone who wants to make a difference in the environment, here are the ways you can help the earth by supporting native plants:

Get involved even without a yard!

Learn the natives + invasives in your area

Becoming familiar with what plants are native and invasive in your area is key to creating a healthy ecosystem. That way you can recognize what plants to support and what plants will need to be replaced.

Advocacy

Contact local nurseries to encourage them to stop selling invasive species and sell more natives. 

Write or call local officials to advocate for laws that support native plant yards

Advocate to your local park to plant natives instead of having a mown lawn.

Get involved in your community garden

Find your local community gardening space and help to incorporate more native species

Incorporating native species support beneficial species and pollinators

In urban areas, rooftop gardens can decrease the amount of water runoff

Donate to your local native plant society

PA native plant society is a non-profit volunteer organization that advocates for native plants and their habitats

You can also volunteer and become a member to support their mission
